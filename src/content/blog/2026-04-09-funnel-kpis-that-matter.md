# The 4 Funnel Metrics That Actually Predict Revenue (And 2 That Don’t)

Marketing dashboards are often cluttered with numbers that feel important but don't drive decisions. Let's cut through the noise and focus on the four metrics that directly correlate with revenue.

## 1. Conversion Rate by Funnel Stage
Overall conversion rate is too broad. You need stage‑by‑stage analysis: lead magnet opt‑in → nurture engagement → sales page visit → purchase. Each stage has its own optimization levers. A low opt‑in rate points to a weak offer or headline; a low sales‑page conversion points to copy or pricing issues.

## 2. Time‑to‑Conversion
How many days from first touch to purchase? This metric tells you how long your nurture sequence needs to be. If most conversions happen in 7 days, a 14‑day sequence may have slack you can tighten.

## 3. Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) by Source
Not all traffic is equal. Track CPA by lead source (organic, paid social, email, referral). You'll find that some channels deliver cheap leads that never convert, while others deliver fewer but more qualified leads.

## 4. Funnel Drop‑Off Rate
Where are you losing people? Identify the largest drop‑off point between stages and focus your optimization there first. A 5% improvement at the biggest leak is worth more than a 20% improvement at a minor one.

## The 2 Vanity Metrics to Ignore
- **Total page views:** High traffic means nothing if it doesn't convert.
- **Email open rate alone:** An open without a click is a vanity stat. Track click‑through rate and conversion from click instead.

**Action step:** Open your analytics right now and map out your funnel stages. Which stage has the steepest drop‑off? Start there.
