# The Automated Referral Engine: How Top Agents Get 30% of Their Business From Past Clients

The average real estate agent gets about 20% of their business from referrals, yet the top 10% of producers get over 50%. The difference? They don't rely on luck—they build a system.

## The Post‑Closing Follow‑Up

The first 90 days after closing are critical. Your clients are excited about their new home, and their network knows it. This is your window to ask for referrals while the experience is fresh.

Sequence:
- **Day 1 after closing:** Celebration email + request for a review on Google or Zillow.
- **Day 30:** “How's the home?” email. Offer a small gift card as a thank you.
- **Day 60:** New‑neighborhood tips (local restaurants, services). Keep it helpful.
- **Day 90:** Soft referral request: “Do you know anyone else thinking of making a move? I'd love to help.”

## The Quarterly Check‑In

Set up automated emails every 3 months:
- **Month 3:** “Spring market update for your area.”
- **Month 6:** “Home anniversary—congrats on 6 months!”
- **Month 9:** “School district update” (if relevant).
- **Month 12:** Annual recap with a direct referral ask.

## Making the Ask Easy

Don't make clients think about *how* to refer you. Include a one‑click link to a simple form: “Know someone? Just enter their email, and I'll take it from there.” Remove all friction.

## Tracking Referrals

Tag referred leads in your CRM. Track which past clients refer the most, and nurture those relationships extra. A handwritten note once a year can cement a lifetime referral partner.

**Start today:** Pull a list of your last 10 closed clients. Are they receiving any automated follow‑up? If not, pick one email from the sequence above and send it manually. Then build the system so it runs on its own.
