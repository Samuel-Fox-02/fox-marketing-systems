# SMS Compliance in 2026: TCPA, 10DLC, and What You Actually Need to Know

Text message marketing has unmatched engagement—open rates above 90% and response rates 7x higher than email. But with that power comes serious legal responsibility. Non‑compliance can mean fines of $500–$1,500 *per text*. Here's what you need to get right.

## The Three Pillars of SMS Compliance

### 1. Consent (TCPA and CTIA)
Before you send a single marketing text, you must have **express written consent**. A checkbox that says “I agree to receive promotional messages” with clear disclosure of frequency and opt‑out instructions is the standard. Silence or pre‑checked boxes are not consent.

### 2. Identification
Every message must identify your business. Use a consistent sender name or short code. The first message in any campaign should set expectations: “Hi, you’re now receiving order updates from Fox Mktg. Reply STOP to opt out.”

### 3. Opt‑Out Mechanism
Recipients must be able to unsubscribe instantly by replying STOP, END, CANCEL, UNSUBSCRIBE, or QUIT. Your system must process opt‑outs immediately. A single unwanted text can trigger complaints and carrier blocks.

## 10DLC (10‑Digit Long Code)
If you're in the US and sending A2P (application‑to‑person) messages from a standard phone number, you need to register your brand and campaign with The Campaign Registry. Unregistered traffic is heavily filtered or blocked. Registration is straightforward and costs a few dollars per campaign.

## What to Avoid
- **Buying lists of phone numbers:** Never. Consent must be directly given to you.
- **Sending promotional messages to transactional opt‑ins:** If a customer provided their number for shipping updates only, don't send them a discount offer.
- **Ignoring time‑of‑day restrictions:** Only send between 8 AM and 9 PM in the recipient's time zone.

## Quick Audit Checklist
- [ ] Is opt‑in language clear and unchecked by default?
- [ ] Do you send an immediate confirmation text after opt‑in?
- [ ] Is “Reply STOP to unsubscribe” included in every message?
- [ ] Is your brand registered for 10DLC?
- [ ] Are you honoring time‑of‑day restrictions?

**Bottom line:** Treat SMS as a privilege, not a right. Respect the inbox, and your customers will reward you with engagement that no other channel can match.
