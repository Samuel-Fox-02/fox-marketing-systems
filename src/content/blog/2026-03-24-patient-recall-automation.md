# How a Single Automated Recall Flow Increased Dental Rebookings by 40%

The most underrated revenue driver in healthcare is the recall. Patients who haven't visited in 6–12 months represent thousands in potential production per year—yet most practices only reach out manually, inconsistently, or not at all.

## The System That Works

A recall flow is not a single email. It's a timed sequence that combines email and SMS to gently guide patients back into the chair.

**Step 1: Identify Lapsed Patients**
Segment patients by last visit date. Start with the 6‑month mark for hygiene recalls, 12‑month for general exams, and 18+ months for a win‑back with a special offer.

**Step 2: Email #1 — Friendly Reminder**
Trigger 5 months and 2 weeks after the last visit. The email should be warm and personal: “It's been a while—and your smile deserves the best. Schedule your next cleaning with one click.”

**Step 3: SMS Reminder**
Send an SMS 2 days after the first email if no action is taken. Keep it brief: “Hi [Name], it's time for your next cleaning. Book here: [link].” SMS open rates are over 95%, making this the most effective nudge.

**Step 4: Final Email with Urgency**
A week later, send one more email with a gentle deadline: “Two appointment slots left this month. Grab yours before they're gone.”

## The Results
Practices that implement a structured recall sequence see rebooking rates jump from around 30% to 70% or more. The key is consistency. Automation ensures no patient falls through the cracks.

**Action step:** Pull a list of patients last seen 6+ months ago. Pick your top 50. Send them a personal email today—even a manual one. Then build the automated system so you never have to do it manually again.
