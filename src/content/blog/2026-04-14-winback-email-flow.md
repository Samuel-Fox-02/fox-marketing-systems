# The 3‑Email Winback Flow That Reactivates 12% of Lapsed Customers

It's 5x more expensive to acquire a new customer than to retain an existing one. Yet most brands ignore customers who haven't purchased in 90+ days. A targeted winback flow can reactivate a surprising percentage—often 10‑15%—without slashing your margins.

## When to Trigger
Set the flow to start 60‑90 days after the last purchase or last email engagement, depending on your product's purchase cycle.

## Email 1: The Reminder
**Subject:** *We’ve missed you — here’s what’s new*  
**Content:** A warm, personal note showcasing what’s changed since they last visited. Highlight new products, features, or content. No hard sell yet.

## Email 2: The Value Drop
**Subject:** *Still thinking about [product]?*  
**Content:** Reference their past interactions. If they browsed a category, show similar items. Include a soft incentive—maybe a small gift with purchase rather than a discount.

## Email 3: The Last Chance
**Subject:** *Before we part ways…*  
**Content:** Offer a modest incentive (free shipping, 10% off) with a 48‑hour expiry. Make it clear this is a final attempt, and ask if they'd like to stay on the list. Respect their choice.

## Key Rules
- Never send to unengaged contacts older than 6 months without a re‑opt‑in.
- Track which customers re‑engage and move them back into your regular segments.
- Use this as an opportunity to clean your list—if they don't respond, suppress them.

**One final tip:** A winback flow is not a set‑it‑and‑forget‑it asset. Review the data quarterly and tweak your messaging.
