# HubSpot vs. Klaviyo vs. ActiveCampaign: Which ESP Fits Your Business?

Choosing the wrong email service provider costs you more than just money—it costs you missed revenue from clunky automations and poor deliverability. I've used all three extensively, and here's where they shine and struggle.

## Klaviyo — Best for E‑Commerce
If you run a Shopify or WooCommerce store, Klaviyo is the undisputed king. Its deep integration with e‑commerce platforms lets you build revenue‑driven flows based on actual customer behavior: abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post‑purchase upsells. The flow builder is intuitive, and the predictive analytics are excellent.

**Pros:** Powerful segmentation, pre‑built e‑commerce flows, great deliverability.  
**Cons:** Can be expensive at high list sizes, limited B2B features.  
**Verdict:** Go with Klaviyo if you're a product‑based business doing $50k+/month.

## ActiveCampaign — Best for B2B and Service Businesses
ActiveCampaign excels at lead scoring, CRM‑integrated pipelines, and sophisticated automation maps. If you're nurturing leads over weeks or months, the automation builder is unmatched.

**Pros:** Deep automation logic, built‑in CRM, excellent for long sales cycles.  
**Cons:** UI can feel overwhelming, e‑commerce features are good but not best‑in‑class.  
**Verdict:** Ideal for coaches, agencies, and B2B SaaS companies.

## HubSpot — Best for All‑In‑One Marketing
HubSpot's Marketing Hub is more than email. It includes a CRM, landing pages, forms, ads, and social tools. The free tier is generous but limits automation.

**Pros:** Fully integrated marketing suite, great for teams, detailed reporting.  
**Cons:** Expensive at scale, automation can be rigid compared to ActiveCampaign.  
**Verdict:** Use HubSpot if you need one tool for everything and have the budget.

**Final thought:** The best ESP is the one you'll actually use to its full potential. Most businesses only scratch the surface of their platform's capabilities.
